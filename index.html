DAY 01
Tuesday, May 19

Arrival — Narita to Kyoto

☀️ 90°F / 67°FKyotoHot day, warm eveningYou arrive late — still warm out
14:50Transit

Land at Narita International Airport

Clear customs — allow 45–60 minutes. You already have the Welcome Suica app set up on your phone. Load ¥10,000+ once you land (top up with your credit card in the app). Works on trains, buses, taxis, convenience stores — everything.

~4:00 pmFood

Kineya Mugimaru — Halal Udon

Terminal 1, 5F Central Building, before security. Halal certified, fresh noodles made daily. First real meal in Japan — don't skip it.

NoteConfirm which terminal when you land. Budget 30 minutes. The next leg is 3.5 hours of trains — eat slowly.
~5:00 pmTransit

Narita → Kyoto

Narita Express → Tokyo Station (~60 min) → Shinkansen Hikari → Kyoto Station (~2.5 hrs). ETA Kyoto ~8:30–9pm.

NoteSit on the right side, seats D or E, for Mt. Fuji views on clear days.

Kyoto Station → Hotel: Hotel shuttle stops at 7:45pm — you'll arrive too late for it. Take a taxi or use the GO app from Kyoto Station to ANA Crowne Plaza (~10 min, ~¥1,500).

Check into ANA Crowne Plaza Kyoto (Nijojo-Mae, Horikawa-Dori). Drop bags. Tonight is easy.

NoteFor every other day: the hotel runs a free shuttle to/from Kyoto Station every 15–20 min, 8:00am–7:45pm. Pick-up is at the Hachijo exit (south/Shinkansen side) near Hoshino Coffee. No reservation needed. Use it for all your day trips.
~9:30 pmPlaces

Fushimi Inari at Night

Fushimi Inari Taisha — open 24 hours. At night the torii gates are lit and nearly empty. A completely different energy from daytime — eerie, quiet, magical. Taxi or GO app from the hotel (~15 min), or subway from Nijojo-mae to Fushimi-Inari (~20 min).

Walk the lower and mid-mountain gates for 30–45 minutes. Don't attempt the full summit at night.

NoteTaxi or GO app back to the hotel after. You'll be tired — keep it to 30–45 min and head back.
~10:30 pmPlaces

Gentle arrival evening

Pontocho Alley — lantern-lit narrow alley along the Kamo River. Perfect first Kyoto impression.

Kamo River — sit on the banks. Locals here even late at night.

Gion district — short atmospheric walk. Don't stress sightseeing tonight.

Light snack if hungry
  • Honolu Ramen Kyoto Gion — wagyu beef ramen 🟢 Halal
  • Men-ya Inoichi Ramen — rich chicken broth
  • Ramen Uzu — Michelin vegan 🟢 Vegan (no meat)
  • 7-Eleven or Lawson — genuinely valid tonight
DAY 02
Wednesday, May 20

Kyoto — Nishiki, Fushimi Inari, Kiyomizudera, Gion

☀️ 89°F / 69°FKyotoHot — stay hydrated on the Fushimi Inari hikeSunscreen + water essential
8:30 amFood

Breakfast at Nishiki Market

Nishiki Market — Kyoto's kitchen. Most stalls open by 9am. Pickles, tofu skewers, tamagoyaki, grilled mochi, fresh soy milk. Eat your way through as breakfast.

Gion Ichiho — matcha-covered strawberry daifuku. One of the best things you'll eat. ⏰ Gone by mid-morning — get there early while you're at the market.

NoteSubway from Nijojo-mae → Karasuma-Oike → Tozai Line → Kyoto-Shiyakusho-mae (~10 min). Nishiki is a 3 min walk south.
Morning coffee near Nishiki
  • Weekenders Coffee — minimalist specialty roaster, 5 min walk
  • Onn Kyoto — quiet neighborhood pour-over
10:00 amPlaces

Fushimi Inari Taisha — daytime visit

You saw it at night on Day 1 — the daytime experience is completely different. 10,000 vermillion torii gates, vibrant in daylight. Walk the lower and mid gates — no need to hike to the summit.

Getting there: Keihan Line from Gion-Shijo → Fushimi-Inari Station (~5 min). Allow about 45 min to walk the gates and take photos.

NoteIt'll be busier than your night visit — but the torii glow in sunlight. The street stalls near the entrance have grilled quail eggs and tamagoyaki.
11:30 amPlaces

Kiyomizudera area

Kiyomizudera — the wooden stage view is extraordinary. It'll be busy by late morning but still worth it.

Ninenzaka & Sannenzaka — stone-paved streets below the temple. Best old-Kyoto walking.

Engraved chopsticks shop — next to the former Ghibli Store near Kiyomizudera. Made while you wait.

Hokanji Temple (Yasaka Pagoda) — five-story pagoda, essential photo from the Ninenzaka lane.

Lunch
  • Tendon Makino Kyoto Teramachi — crispy tempura rice bowl
  • Shushiki Kyoto — creative Japanese small plates
  • Curry & Tempura Koisus — curry + tempura combo
  • Sushi no Musashi — conveyor belt sushi
  • Im Donut? — famous brioche donuts, lines are long
1:30 pmPlaces

Philosopher's Path + Ginkakuji + Maruyama

Philosopher's Path — 2km canal walk. Calm and photogenic.

Higashiyama Jisho-ji (Ginkakuji) — Silver Pavilion and raked sand garden. Less crowded than Kinkakuji.

Maruyama Park — wander through on the way to Gion.

Matcha / coffee break
  • Maccha House — matcha tiramisu
  • Nanaya — matcha gelato (7 intensity levels)
  • Totaro Matcha — matcha drinks + sweets
  • Chabako — cozy matcha tea house

Ceremony starts at 3:30pm. ~1 hour. You'll be done around 4:30pm.

Osaka — Market, Namba, Castle, Shinsekai

⛅ 81°F / 67°FOsakaWarm, partly cloudy — comfortable walking weatherUmbrella just in case
8:30 amTransit

Kyoto → Osaka — Hankyu Line, ~40 minutes

Subway from Nijojo-mae → Karasuma-Oike → transfer to Tozai Line → Sanjo Keihan (~10 min), then walk to Kawaramachi Station. Hankyu Kyoto Line from Kawaramachi → Osaka Umeda (~40 min). No Shinkansen needed — cheap and fast.

~9:30 amCoffee

Glitch Coffee Osaka — with friends

Glitch Coffee Osaka — Japan's best single-origin light roast. Festival Tower West 1F, 3-2-4 Nakanoshima, Kita-ku. Opens 8am weekdays.

Getting there: From Osaka Umeda (where the Hankyu drops you), take the Midosuji Line 1 stop to Yodoyabashi Station (~3 min). Glitch is a 5 min walk from exit 4.

NoteConfirm meeting time with friends. After coffee, head south toward Kuromon Market — it's ~15 min by subway from Yodoyabashi.
~11:00 amPlaces

Kuromon Market + Shitenno-ji

Kuromon Ichiba Market — Osaka's kitchen. Fresh fish, wagyu, oysters, fruit. Eat your way through. Closes mid-afternoon — don't push this too late.

Shitenno-ji — one of Japan's oldest temples. Skip if running behind from morning coffee.

12:30 pmFood

Namba — Hozenji, Dotonburi, Glico Man

Namba Yasaka Jinja — giant lion-head shrine. Dramatic photo spot.

Hozenji Yokocho — moss-covered tiny alley. Osaka's most atmospheric hidden spot.

Dotonburi — canal walk, takoyaki, okonomiyaki, Glico Man photo.

Lunch — pick one

  • Ichiran Ramen Namba — solo booth ramen ritual ⚠️ Has no-pork option at Dotonbori location
  • Onigiri Gorichan Namba — stuffed rice balls, huge portions
  • Kawafuki Honten Udon — verify broth
  • Wagyu Idaten — wagyu beef bowls, fast + affordable ⚠️ Not halal certified
  • Maguro Ittetsu — sushi
2:30 pmShopping

America Mura + Shinsaibashi + Ebisubashi

One connected walkable zone.

Vintage / resale

Osaka Castle at night + Shinsekai

Osaka Castle — lit up at night. Castle park beautiful at dusk.

Shinsekai — retro 1960s neighbourhood. Eat kushikatsu (fried skewers).

Umeda Sky Building — floating garden observatory, ~30 min from Namba. If energy allows.

Arashiyama — Bamboo, Monkeys, River Boat

🌤 84°F / 66°FKyoto / ArashiyamaWarm + clear — great for the river boatEarly morning cool, hot by noon
6:30 amPlaces

Arashiyama Bamboo Forest

NoteHotel shuttle doesn't start until 8am — too late for bamboo. Use the GO app to hail a taxi from the hotel at 6am (~20 min ride, ~¥3,000–4,000). Worth it.

Must go before 7:30am. By 9am it's shoulder-to-shoulder. Before 7:30am it feels like a different world.

Tenryuji Temple garden — opens 8:30am, just beside the bamboo. Stunning pond garden with mountain backdrop.

Coffee on arrival
  • ★ % Arabica Arashiyama — iconic spot with river view
8:30 amPlaces

Monkey Park

Arashiyama Iwatayama Monkey Park — 20-min uphill hike to ~120 wild macaques. You feed them from inside a cage. Panoramic Kyoto views from the top. Allow ~1.5 hours total.

11:00 amFood

Early Lunch in Arashiyama

Eat early — you need to be at Kameoka by 12:45pm for the boat.

Arashiyama → Kameoka for boat ride

JR Sagano Line from Saga-Arashiyama Station → Kameoka Station (~8 min). From Kameoka Station, follow signs to the boat launch — it's about a 10 min walk.

Arrive by 12:45pm. Your ticket is for the 1:00pm departure.

!Don't be late — boats leave on time. Leave the restaurant by noon at the latest.

Hozugawa River Boat Ride

2-hour gorge boat ride down the Hozu River from Kameoka back to Arashiyama. Scenic rapids, forested canyon, incredibly peaceful. You end up right back where you started in Arashiyama around 3pm.
